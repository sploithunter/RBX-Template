## Definition

**Up-close single-target DPS** — runs in, claws/bites. The **most populous line** (every origin
fields at least one melee body) and therefore **the line most at risk of reskinning**: it's almost
all Commons/Uncommons, which by design stay **vanilla**. This is the line where the *graftable
economy-power escape hatch* matters most.

## Where melee lives (every origin has a body)

- **🔥 Fire** — the **Common melee** (Cherub/Imp) *and* the **Uncommon bruiser** (Lion, a melee
  tank-DPS). Two melee bodies per Fire egg.
- **❄️ Ice** — the **Common melee** (Hare/Seal/Marten/Ermine).
- **🌿 Grass** — the **Common melee** (Hare/Hart), plus an **L5 bruiser** (Eden/Void Badger).
- **🏜️ Desert** — **L1 only:** the evasion Jackal (Mirage/Phantom). From L2 up Desert goes pure
  support, so it drops its melee body.

## Designated-power progression

Melee is **deliberately the thin-mechanic line** — these are squad bodies + stack fodder, fine to
reskin vertically. Two light signatures only:

| Slot | Verb | Debut | Escalation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Common melee (all origins) | **plain hit** → small **cleave** | L4 | L4 cleave 2 adjacent → L5 cleave 3 |
| Bruiser (Fire Lion, Grass Badger) | **lifesteal on hit** | L3 | L3 small leech → L5 leech + brief rage (atk↑) |

Everything Common at L1–L3 stays vanilla on purpose.

## Overlaps with other lines

- **↔ Blaster:** same origins, opposite range. Fire egg pairs melee (Cherub) + bruiser (Lion) +
  blaster (Phoenix) — melee is the "get-in" half of Fire's damage identity.
- **↔ Tank:** the **bruiser** straddles melee↔tank (durable melee); the Grass **Badger** (L5) is a
  bruiser sharing the grass egg with the tank line. The line blurs here by design.
- **↔ Dragon:** the **Ice dragons (L2, Aurora/Rimewraith) are melee** — the only time the dragon line
  overlaps melee. A melee dragon that also freezes (see controller overlap).
- **Within-line (the reskin risk — biggest of any line):** ~20 common-melee bodies that are
  mechanically identical. **This is where the graftable economy power earns its keep** — a "+coins"
  or "+luck" on a mid-layer melee turns a forgettable body into a keeper without new combat tech.
  Recommended: from L3+, sprinkle economy/utility powers across *some* melee commons so the line has
  texture even though its combat stays simple.

## Build state

Nothing authored. Cleave/lifesteal are future config. The near-term lever for this line is **not**
combat mechanics — it's grafting economy powers onto a handful of deeper-layer melee commons to break
the reskin monotony cheaply.
